How did I get here?

Initially, I wanted to attend the University of Houston to major in industrial design. I was eager to learn about product design and everything related to making functional products for viable consumption. Therefore, I aspired to become an industrial designer to explore those interests. I got into the University of Houston. However, I did not make it into its industrial design program. The attendance costs were too expensive to hope I got into the program the following year while I attended the school. I applied to the University of Central Florida (UCF) as a pivot to my dreams of studying industrial design at an out-of-state university.

I ultimately chose to attend UCF because of the different domains offered by the university's psychology program. I decided to major in psychology with a concentration on human factors. The human factors route provided an opportunity to learn about product usability and user experience from a psychological perspective. Eventually, I realized I wanted to pursue a career in user experience (UX) design instead of industrial design. There were some classes related to user experience that I could take if I decided to minor in digital media. With that, the decision to minor in digital media soon followed. That is how I got here as a UCF student majoring in psychology with a minor in digital media.

The Lessons I Learned
  1. Don't be afraid to ask questions or for help. The worst response you could get is probably no. You never know what kind of insight or help you might get without asking.
  2. Remain in the present. Living in the moment is challenging. However, you owe it to yourself to appreciate the moment and live for the now.
  3. There's only so much you can control, so try not to worry about the things you cannot control. It saves you from a lot of stress and anxiety.
  4. It never hurts to express gratitude. I am grateful for everything that has got me here, and this acknowledgment helps me appreciate life much more.

Final Thoughts

My time at UCF has been relatively short. I am graduating this spring semester. I only spent three years here, so graduating from UCF feels odd. I am entirely grateful for the experiences that led me to graduate a bit quicker. However, it is somewhat bittersweet. I did not savor the college experience as much as I wanted to. The ongoing COVID pandemic and my persistent anxiety dampened my excitement and engagement surrounding college. I strictly focused on my academics, which has significantly benefited me. Though, I did not push myself enough to connect with other people. I am a bit regretful about that. My journey at UCF often feels like an exhausting blur of time. I did not make any fruitful connections with anyone. Instead, I became more introspective. I learned more about myself as a student at UCF, so I appreciate the three years of introspection and personal development. I am eager for the next chapter of my life, and it could not be possible without my journey at UCF.
